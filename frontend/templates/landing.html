KEY MILESTONES

A Legacy of Firsts

1975
Aryabhata — India's First Satellite
Launched on a Soviet Intercosmos rocket, Aryabhata was India's debut in space — named after the legendary 5th-century mathematician and astronomer.
1980
SLV-3 — First Indigenous Rocket
India became only the 7th nation to build and launch its own orbital rocket, successfully deploying the Rohini satellite from Sriharikota.
2008
Chandrayaan-1 — Water on the Moon
ISRO's Moon Impact Probe confirmed water molecules on the lunar surface — a discovery of immense global scientific significance.
2014
Mangalyaan — Mars on First Attempt
India became the first nation to reach Martian orbit on its maiden attempt, joining an elite club of space agencies at a fraction of usual costs.
2017
PSLV-C37 — 104 Satellites, One Rocket
A single PSLV simultaneously deployed 104 satellites — setting a world record and cementing ISRO's reputation as a premium launch partner.
2023
Chandrayaan-3 — South Pole Landing
Vikram lander touched down near the lunar south pole on August 23 — a world first. India became only the 4th country to soft-land on the Moon.
